# Site Positioning & Messaging Overhaul

## Problem Frame

The Innovise website currently positions the company as a generic custom software consultancy serving "startups to Fortune 50." This range is so wide it speaks to no one specifically. The actual target customer — SMBs without an established internal engineering team — is not addressed by the hero, the services framing, or the engagement model descriptions.

Four real buying triggers exist for Innovise's ideal client, and none are reflected in the current copy:

1. **No internal dev team** — "we need someone to build this"
2. **Existing team, needs bandwidth** — "we have engineers but we're stuck / behind"
3. **Need leadership and experience** — "we need senior guidance, not just execution"
4. **Scaling fast on a new project** — "we have momentum and need to accelerate"

Additionally, the most compelling differentiator — that Innovise is willing to help clients build their own permanent internal team — does not appear anywhere on the site. This is a rare trust signal that directly counters the biggest fear SMB buyers have about agencies: creating dependency.

## Competitive Intelligence

Research from April 2026 across competitor sites, SMB forums, and market data. Informs service catalog decisions and what the messaging must counter.

**What's commoditized — avoid leading with these:**

- Standard website builds and e-commerce integrations
- "We use agile" / "We use AI tools" (said by everyone)
- Pure staff augmentation / body-shop billing
- Open-ended time-and-materials without a scope anchor
- Speed-to-market as a solo claim (AI compressed timelines industry-wide)

**What SMBs say they're not getting from agencies (primary unmet needs):**

- Senior engineers actually doing the work — bait-and-switch (seniors in sales, juniors on the build) is the #1 cited complaint
- Domain understanding before code — agencies that ask about business outcomes, not just stack
- Proactive risk flagging — SMBs lack internal PM/CTO to catch issues before they escalate
- Measurable outcomes — 60% of SMBs cite "lack of ROI" as primary reason for leaving agencies
- Continuity post-launch — most agencies disappear after go-live

**Engagement models gaining traction with SMBs:**

- Paid discovery sprint / fixed-price entry product (before any long commitment)
- Fixed-budget scope-controlled delivery (Atomic Object's model; reduces budget anxiety)
- Fractional CTO / embedded technical leadership retainer (fast-growing standalone category)
- Month-to-month retainers with no lock-in
- AI workflow automation audit (emerging adjacent service — 62% of SMBs using AI tools reactively)

**How top boutique competitors position:**

- **Thoughtbot:** "The change we leave behind" — mentors client teams so they're better after the engagement. Adds fractional product and technical leadership.
- **Test Double:** Embeds with teams, anti-AI-slop, business outcomes over hype. Explicitly named "no cheap butts in seats" positioning.
- **Atomic Object:** Fixed-budget scope-controlled. "How do we get the most value from the remaining budget?" every week.
- **8th Light:** "Vibe-to-Value" — prototype to production-ready agentic AI in 8 weeks. Hard anti-prototype-theater stance.

**Emerging service opportunities (from ideation session):**

1. **Fractional Embedded Technical Partner** — monthly retainer, Loren directly involved, not advisory-only
2. **Team-Building as a Named Service** — structured 6–12 month path that ends with the client having their own internal team
3. **Productized Entry Audit** — fixed-price ($3–8k) build/buy/risk brief, no scoping conversation required
4. **AI Workflow Automation Audit** — maps manual workflows, identifies automation candidates, delivers prioritized roadmap
5. **The Rescue Retainer** — structured engagement for SMBs abandoned post-launch by a previous agency
6. **Offshore Repatriation** — specialist track for companies stuck in a failed offshore relationship
7. **The Guild Model** — vetted network of senior independents operating under the Innovise brand (solves founder-dependency ceiling)

**What the site should remove or rethink:**

- "Custom website builds" as a standalone package — commoditized in 2026; folds into general dev engagement
- Monthly maintenance retainer in its current form ("software support on demand") — undifferentiated; needs reframing as a strategic advisory retainer or dropped in favor of the fractional partner model

## Requirements

**Hero & Top-Level Positioning**

- R1. The hero headline must speak to SMBs in one of the four buying trigger situations above, not to an undifferentiated audience of all company sizes.
- R2. The hero subheadline or supporting copy must establish the "embedded partner" positioning — Innovise functions like an internal team, not a vendor.
- R3. The hero must remove the "startups to Fortune 50" framing entirely. It dilutes the SMB focus and signals a lack of specificity to the target buyer.
- R4. Both CTA labels in the hero ("Let's build together" and "See our services") may be retained but should be reviewed for alignment with the revised positioning.

**Embedded Partner Framing**

- R5. At least one section beyond the hero must reinforce the embedded team dynamic — that Innovise integrates with the client's team, tools, and culture rather than operating as an external supplier.
- R6. The "How We Work" section must surface what makes the process feel like an internal team: direct communication, visibility into progress, lack of handoffs. The current three steps (Discover / Build / Deliver & support) are structurally sound but the descriptions read as generic agency-speak.

**Trust Signal: Helping You Build Your Own Team**

- R7. The "help you build your own team" message is placed in the Founder section — specifically in Loren's profile copy as the closing statement. No new section or structural change is required.
- R8. This message must be framed as a differentiator: Innovise does not create dependency. The goal is the client's long-term success, even if that means they eventually need Innovise less.

**Boutique Quality Signal**

- R9. The copy must signal boutique quality — senior engineers, no bait-and-switch to junior staff, no offshore handoffs — through specificity rather than stated claims. Using the word "boutique" is acceptable if it reads as confident rather than defensive in context; the quality signal should not depend on it.
- R10. The tagline or brand descriptor ("Custom Software Consulting") should be revisited. It is accurate but undifferentiated. The revised label should hint at the embedded/partner model.

**Scope of SMB Messaging**

- R11. The revised copy must address all four buying triggers somewhere in the page flow, so a visitor in any of those situations recognizes themselves. This does not require four separate sections — it can be achieved through a combined subheadline, a brief list, or a well-written package description.

**Services Section**

- R15. Remove or de-emphasize any service that is now commoditized and undifferentiated — specifically "custom website builds" as a standalone named package. If retained, it must be framed around outcomes (e.g., modern web experiences, conversion-focused builds) rather than as a generic offering.
- R16. The services section copy must counter the #1 SMB complaint (bait-and-switch) through specificity — senior engineers doing the actual work, not managing junior output.
- R17. "Technical leadership & scaling" should be reframed to more explicitly include the fractional/embedded leadership angle — the market now has a named category (Fractional CTO) that Innovise's founder profile maps directly to.

**Packages Section**

- R18. The "Monthly maintenance" package must be reframed. "Software support on demand" is undifferentiated. Reframe toward ongoing strategic partnership — proactive risk flagging, architecture advisory, access to Loren — rather than reactive support.
- R19. The packages section must introduce or hint at the entry audit product as the lowest-commitment way to start a relationship with Innovise. A prospect should see a clear path from "I'm not sure what I need" → audit → larger engagement.
- R20. The "Development teams" package description (currently the featured/hero package) should explicitly name the team-building exit ramp: Innovise can serve as the team until the client is ready to hire permanently.

**Team Section Restructure**

- R12. The team section is restructured from a 4-person card grid to two distinct blocks:
  1. **Founder block** — Loren Anderson only, with a philosophy-forward bio (see approved copy below). No accolade listing; framed around how he engages clients and what they can expect.
  2. **Collective team block** — A prose description of the broader team (senior developers, UX designers, product strategists, mentors) without individual names or cards. Emphasizes seniority, fit-to-project assembly, and experienced practitioners only.
- R13. Sam Zamor, Jim Reynolds, and Austin Reynolds are removed from the team section entirely for this version of the site.
- R14. The founder bio must not reference specific client names, user counts, or named engagements. Credibility signals (scale, complexity, team-building) are conveyed through category language, not specifics.

## Success Criteria

- An SMB founder or operations lead with no internal dev team reads the hero and immediately understands that Innovise could function as their engineering department.
- A CTO at an SMB with an existing team reads the page and sees a partner who augments rather than displaces their team.
- A visitor who has been burned by an agency before finds at least one trust signal that distinguishes Innovise from a typical dev shop (embedded model, senior team, willingness to help build internal capacity).
- Boutique quality comes through — ideally through specificity (senior engineers, direct access, no handoffs) rather than the word itself, though using "boutique" explicitly is acceptable if it reads as confident in context.
- The team section no longer contains lorem ipsum placeholder content.

## Scope Boundaries

- Layout, design, and component structure changes are **in scope** for the team section restructure (R12), but limited to the `src/components/Team.astro` file only. All other section layout is out of scope.
- Adding new page sections (case studies, testimonials, blog) is out of scope for this pass.
- The commented-out "Analytics & dashboards" can be deleted. I don't think it adds any real value.
- No new pages are in scope; this is a single-page pass.
- Founder bio must not reference specific client names, named platforms, or precise metrics (user counts, team headcounts).

## Key Decisions

- **Positioning direction:** "Embedded Partner" — Innovise is your engineering team, not a vendor you hire. The growth arc (helping clients build their own permanent team) is folded in as the closing line of the founder bio.
- **Target audience:** SMBs — companies without an established internal engineering team, or with teams that need bandwidth, leadership, or acceleration. "Startups to Fortune 50" is removed from the hero.
- **Industry:** Agnostic — no vertical focus to signal.
- **Differentiator emphasis:** Embeddedness + senior quality + client-first (willing to work toward the client not needing us).
- **Team section:** Restructured to founder-only named profile + collective team description. Individual cards for Sam Zamor, Jim Reynolds, and Austin Reynolds are removed.
- **R7 placement resolved:** The "help you build your own team" trust signal lives in the founder bio as the closing statement — no new structural element required.
