# SEO findings — baseline read (2026-05-14)

Companion to [`baseline-2026-05-14.md`](./baseline-2026-05-14.md). The baseline is mechanical; this is the read.

## Top-line: small numbers, real direction

In raw terms the site is still small (28 organic clicks / 2,583 impressions / 296 sessions over 90 days). What matters is the *shape* of growth: clicks +180%, impressions +20%, CTR +134%, sessions +55%, pageviews +362%, avg engagement per session 7.8s → 66.4s. Something materially improved on-page in the comparison window — likely the recent copy + image overhaul. Worth noting and worth not breaking.

## What Google has decided you're about (and what to do about it)

Looking at the 25 top queries by impressions, the picture is unambiguous: **Google has indexed Innovise primarily as a brand-name destination.** 19 of the top 25 are some variant of the company name (innovise, innovise it, innovise technologies, innovise software, innovise company, innovise solutions, etc., plus misspellings like incovise/innovice). Only 6 are non-brand queries, and 4 of those are fractional-CTO geo terms.

This has two implications.

1. **Brand search is being polluted by another company.** "innovise it" (163 impressions, 0 clicks, position 8.9) and "innovise it llc" (30 impr, position 6.2) are people looking for a different "Innovise IT" entity and not clicking on you. Same with "innovise group", "innovise solutions", "innovise-it". You're scraping low-CTR impressions for someone else's brand. Not actionable in the short term except to (a) make your own brand result unmissable when it does appear, and (b) build out enough unique on-page content that Google stops confusing the entities.

2. **Almost zero unbranded discovery.** Across 90 days there is essentially one non-brand commercial query earning clicks: nothing. The 4 fractional-CTO geo terms (wa, washington, seattle, "find a fractional cto", "hire fractional cto") collectively earned 0 clicks on 24 impressions. `custom software development seattle` showed up once at position 45. `technology audit for small business` showed up once at position 17. **This is the gap.** The site currently doesn't rank for any of the buyer-intent terms it's trying to serve.

## The fractional-CTO tension

marketing-copy.mdc explicitly says fractional CTO "doesn't scale really well … shouldn't be the top-promoted unit." But it is the *only* service category showing demand in GSC right now — every non-brand query in the dataset that isn't a typo is fractional-CTO-shaped. Two reads:

- **Honest read**: the market is asking for what they want, not what you want to sell them. There's a real choice to make: lean into being findable for "fractional CTO WA" (where you're already at position 6.8, near page 1) and use it as a top-of-funnel for buyers who then convert into embedded-team engagements, *or* deliberately suppress the fractional-CTO page in favor of the services you actually want to grow. The current site is doing neither cleanly.
- **No data yet on** MVP-to-production, internal team development, AI engineering — the three services marketing-copy.mdc explicitly wants to grow. These pages exist but have ~zero impressions. Google doesn't yet know they're there in a way that matches buyer queries.

This isn't a copy problem. It's a "Google hasn't indexed your value props as topics" problem. Two things drive that: (a) thin/single page coverage of a topic — no surrounding content reinforcing what each service page is *about*; (b) no inbound links signaling those pages matter. Both are solvable with content. Neither is solvable in a day.

## CTR on the brand term is broken

`innovise` itself: 1,146 impressions, 9 clicks, 0.79% CTR, position 6.6. People are searching your brand and *not finding you on the first try*. Likely causes, in priority order:

1. **You're not first.** Position 6.6 on your own brand name means competing companies (Innovise IT, Innovise Group, etc.) are above you. This is a structural problem fixed by stronger on-page signal (consistent name use, schema markup — which you have — and link signals).
2. **Snippet doesn't earn the click.** Current homepage `<title>` is `Embedded Software Development Team for SMBs | Innovise`, meta description starts "Senior engineers embedded in your business". For someone Googling "innovise" specifically, neither tells them "this is the Innovise you're looking for, the Bellevue WA one with Loren." There's a credible case for putting the founder's name and city into the meta description so brand searchers self-identify.
3. **Domain/branding mismatch.** Domain is goinnovise.com; brand is "Innovise"; legal/full name in schema is "Innovise Technologies." Mild friction but probably not the main driver.

## Pages: the rest of the site is dark

22 of 28 organic clicks land on `/`. The next-most-clicked page is `/services` with 2. Every service detail page combined: 0 clicks. The MVP-to-production page (a deliberate growth target per marketing-copy.mdc): 4 GA sessions in 90 days, 0 engaged. Same for embedded-team, custom-software, internal-team-development, technology-audit. AI-engineering: 1 session, 1 engaged at 136s — interesting outlier worth watching.

What this means: every page below the homepage is essentially invisible to Google right now. The homepage carries the whole site. Marketing copy at the service-page level can be perfect and still not move the business until those pages get crawled, indexed, and linked to from somewhere with authority.

## Traffic shape (GA4)

- **Direct traffic dominates** (234 sessions, 80%). These are people who already know about you — typing in the URL, clicking from email/Discord/LinkedIn DMs, or coming from the Calendly/contact flow. Not SEO-driven. Good for now (it's how a young firm gets started); a ceiling later.
- **Organic search is 31 sessions (10%).** This is the number to grow.
- **Referral is 29 sessions (10%) from 7 users** — looks like a small number of high-engagement referrers. Worth knowing where; the channel grouping doesn't expand it but the next iteration of this report can.
- **Geo anomaly**: 128 sessions from Singapore vs. 55 from Washington. Singapore traffic almost certainly = bots, crawlers, or scrapers; the 1:1 sessions-to-users ratio is the giveaway. Real US traffic is the signal — Washington 55/10, California 16/6, BC/Canada 10/5, Virginia 10/10. The Virginia ratio (1:1) also looks botty. Worth a filter pass on GA4 to exclude bot networks if we're going to use GA4 as a decision signal.
- **Engagement on `/services`: 266s average**. People who hit that page stay 4+ minutes. Tiny sample (7 sessions) but very strong signal. Whatever's on that page is working when people get there.
- **Mobile is 10% of sessions** (31 of 302). Buyer behavior is desktop-first, as expected for B2B technical-services. Doesn't change priorities.
- **Conversions/events**: `form_submit` fires 9 times in 90 days. `form_start` 11. These should be flagged as **conversions** in GA4 (Admin → Events → mark as key event) so we can attribute them to channels. Right now we're literally not measuring lead conversion by source.

## What I'd do, in order

**This week — wins inside the current site**
1. Mark `form_submit` (and any Calendly click event if one exists) as GA4 conversions. 5 minutes. Unlocks attribution forever.
2. Rewrite homepage `<title>` and meta description to put "Bellevue, WA" + "Loren Anderson" + concrete differentiator in the snippet. Branded searchers should self-identify in the SERP without clicking.
3. Add a filter to GA4 to exclude the Singapore/Virginia bot traffic so the dashboard tells the truth.
4. Audit the service pages: title tags, meta descriptions, H1s. marketing-copy.mdc has specific SEO copy decisions for MVP-to-production and internal team development; verify the pages match. (Especially: "Cursor, Bolt, Replit" should appear in the MVP page's first paragraph; "offshore to onshore" should be in the internal-team page's title.) If they're not there yet, that's a copy-only PR.

**Next 2–4 weeks — make Google care about the service pages**
5. Decide the fractional-CTO position: either (a) build out the page to genuinely rank for the WA/Seattle geo terms (you're already at position 6.8 on `fractional cto wa` — that's *very* close to page 1), accepting that buyers come in fractional-shaped and convert into other engagements, or (b) deliberately downplay it and redirect that intent. Currently you're stuck in between.
6. Start a `/insights` collection (Astro content collection). One canonical post on the MVP-to-production rescue story, naming Cursor/Bolt/Replit in the body. One on "from offshore to onshore: how the ed-tech client built a 25-person team." One on the LLM data-extraction proof point. These exist as case studies in the brand doc — they're literally pre-written. Long-form proof posts are how a senior-led firm earns trust at the same time as ranking for the specific buyer intents.
7. Internal linking: every service page should link to (a) the others, (b) the case-study posts, (c) the homepage. Currently the service pages look like silos to Google.

**Ongoing — measurement loop**
8. Run the baseline audit weekly (cron, like the hermes-learnings backup). Diff week-over-week to spot what's moving. I can wire that up next.
9. After 4–6 weeks of content, re-pull GSC and see whether the impression footprint has broadened to non-brand terms. That's the real success metric — not clicks yet, *impressions on the queries we care about*.

## Recommendations I am NOT making (yet)

- Paid search. Premature. Fix indexing and intent capture first; paid amplifies whatever conversion engine you already have.
- A blog "content engine." Specifically *not* what I'm suggesting. The recommendation in #6 is small, specific, founder-voiced posts that reinforce existing services. Not weekly listicles.
- Backlink outreach campaigns. Also premature; needs anchor content to point at.
- Schema changes. Your Layout.astro already emits ProfessionalService + WebSite JSON-LD. Article/CaseStudy schema is worth adding *when* there are articles to mark up.
