# Ideation: Innovise Service Catalog & Product Plan

## Topic Context

**Company:** Innovise — boutique software services firm, embedded partner model ("your engineering team, not a vendor")
**Target:** SMBs in four buying trigger situations: no dev team / need bandwidth / need leadership / scaling fast
**Founder:** Loren Anderson — 15 years full-stack + technical strategy, has built and scaled engineering teams, orchestrated complex migrations
**Current services:** Custom software dev, technical leadership & scaling, system integrations, systems design & architecture, DevOps & cloud, UX & design, technology & security audits
**Current packages:** Development teams, custom website builds, monthly maintenance, technology & security audits
**Key differentiator:** Senior-only, embedded (feel like internal team), willing to help clients build their own team

**Stated constraints:** Senior-only model non-negotiable. Not a staffing agency. Industry agnostic. Willing to help clients reduce dependency on Innovise.

**Named pain points:** No entry-point product for cold prospects. Four buying triggers undifferentiated in packaging. Retainer model commoditized without a stickier anchor. No land-and-expand motion.

**Competitive context (from web research):**

- Fractional CTO is a fast-growing standalone category ($800/month+ retainers)
- AI workflow automation is an emerging adjacent SMB market (62% of SMBs using AI tools reactively)
- Paid discovery sprints replacing direct-to-engagement; fixed-budget models reducing commit anxiety
- Staff aug, T&M, "we use agile" — commoditized and losing to AI-augmented delivery
- Top boutiques (Thoughtbot, Test Double, Atomic Object, 8th Light) lead with outcomes and mentorship, not hours
- Offshore market contracting; nearshore/onshore gaining — SMBs burned by offshore are a real buyer segment
- 60% of SMBs cite "lack of ROI" as primary reason for leaving agencies (vcita, 2025)

## Ranked Ideas

### 1. Fractional Embedded Technical Partner

**Description:** A monthly retainer positioned as neither fractional CTO (too advisory) nor dev agency (too transactional), but as "the senior engineer who joined at founding and never left." Loren is directly involved on every account. Month-to-month, cancel anytime. Scope is deliberately broad: architecture decisions, code review, hiring input, roadmap advice, shipping when needed. Roughly $8k–$18k/month.
**Rationale:** Directly monetizes Loren's profile into the fastest-growing service category in the SMB space. Fractional CTO firms at $800/month can't do this — they advise but don't build. Innovise can do both, which is what SMBs actually need when they lack a technical anchor. Converts the most distinctive Innovise asset (embedded judgment + delivery) into recurring revenue.
**Downsides:** Founder-dependent by design. Loren can sustain a finite number of concurrent accounts; requires the Guild model (#7) to scale.
**Confidence:** 90%
**Complexity:** Low — repackaging and naming what Innovise already does
**Status:** Unexplored

---

### 2. Team-Building as a Named Commercial Service

**Description:** A structured 6–12 month engagement explicitly designed to end with the client having their own internal engineering team. Innovise handles delivery while simultaneously running the client's hiring process: writing specs, sourcing candidates, conducting technical screens, advising on offers. The exit ramp is baked in from day one — not a surprise, not a revenue loss. Optional standalone SKU: "Technical Hiring Partner" where development is additive.
**Rationale:** The most uncontested positioning in the market. No dev shop does this commercially. Loren's team-building experience is directly monetized. The "help you build your own team" differentiator in the site copy becomes a commercial product with a price, a process, and a deliverable.
**Downsides:** Requires a repeatable hiring process playbook. Longer engagements (6–12 months). Exit management is critical — graduated clients should become referral sources.
**Confidence:** 88%
**Complexity:** Medium — requires developing a hiring process product alongside dev delivery
**Status:** Unexplored

---

### 3. The Productized Entry Audit

**Description:** A fixed-price ($3–8k), fixed-scope audit that answers the question most prospects need before committing: "What do I actually need, what risks am I carrying, and should I build, buy, or integrate?" Delivered as a one-page executive brief plus a prioritized roadmap. No embedded proposal for Innovise follow-on work — the roadmap creates natural pipeline without requiring a pitch. Technology & security audits are already offered; this productizes them as a clear, low-friction entry product any SMB can buy without a scoping conversation.
**Rationale:** Removes the biggest cold-prospect conversion barrier. Fixed-price entry products are proven land-and-expand mechanisms (Atomic Object, Thoughtbot Discovery Sprints). The "build or buy" variant is the highest-trust entry point because it removes Innovise's conflict of interest — sometimes the honest answer is "don't build."
**Downsides:** Needs clear scope limits so the audit doesn't become a free strategy session that doesn't convert. Risk of attracting tire-kickers if not priced firmly enough.
**Confidence:** 92%
**Complexity:** Low — audits are already offered; this is productization and positioning
**Status:** Unexplored

---

### 4. AI Workflow Automation Audit

**Description:** A standalone fixed-price engagement that maps the client's existing manual workflows, identifies the highest-leverage automation candidates (n8n, Make, Zapier + custom AI agents), and delivers a prioritized automation roadmap with estimated hours-saved-per-week per item. Optional implementation retainer for the top 2–3 automations follows.
**Rationale:** 62% of SMBs use AI tools reactively without a strategic framework. This is a fast-emerging adjacent market that boutique dev shops are either absorbing or ceding to AI-automation-only firms. Distinct revenue line that doesn't require dev capacity; feeds into or runs independently from dev engagements. Positions Innovise as forward-looking on AI without empty claims.
**Downsides:** Requires genuine expertise in the automation tooling ecosystem (n8n, Make, agentic patterns), not just awareness. Risk of scope creep into custom development without a clear handoff point.
**Confidence:** 80%
**Complexity:** Medium — requires building competency in AI automation specifically
**Status:** Unexplored

---

### 5. The Rescue Retainer

**Description:** A dedicated structured engagement type for SMBs abandoned post-launch or left with a broken codebase. Starts with a structured diagnosis (what's broken, what's dangerous, what's recoverable), produces a prioritized recovery roadmap, offers a clear continuation path. Framing: "we take over where they left you."
**Rationale:** This buyer is in active crisis — faster conversion, real budget authority, high urgency. The previous agency's failure created the pipeline. Senior engineers diagnose unfamiliar codebases quickly, which is the core Innovise credential here. Strong referral source: a company that was burned and then rescued becomes a vocal advocate.
**Downsides:** Inheriting broken codebases carries scope risk — the full damage isn't always visible in the audit phase. Needs contractual limits on what "recovery" means vs. rebuild. Burned clients can sometimes be difficult to work with.
**Confidence:** 85%
**Complexity:** Low — a framed positioning and intake process, not a new capability
**Status:** Unexplored

---

### 6. The Offshore Repatriation Service

**Description:** Specialist positioning for companies stuck in a failed offshore development relationship — deteriorating quality, communication breakdown, code that's hard to maintain. Innovise offers a structured transition: code quality assessment, transition timeline, knowledge transfer, and continuation path with Innovise as the onshore embedded team.
**Rationale:** Loren has direct hands-on experience with this exact problem (offshore-to-onshore transitions). The offshore-to-nearshore shift is accelerating — 42% of large organizations plan to move within 18 months (IDC, 2026). Specific enough that a specialist framing outconverts a generic "we do better work" pitch. The buyer already made the decision; they just need the right firm.
**Downsides:** Smaller TAM than general SMB positioning. Best treated as a named specialization, not a primary brand move.
**Confidence:** 82%
**Complexity:** Low — specialist positioning around existing capabilities
**Status:** Unexplored

---

### 7. The Innovise Guild Model

**Description:** Loren builds a personally-vetted network of senior independent engineers, designers, and product strategists who work under the Innovise brand. Loren remains the strategic and relationship layer on every account; guild members handle execution. The curation itself is the differentiator — clients hire Loren's judgment about who they're working with. No bench, no utilization pressure. Guild members are senior independents, not employees.
**Rationale:** The only path to scale that doesn't compromise the senior-only quality promise. Without it, Innovise's capacity ceiling is Loren's available hours. Enables simultaneous fractional partner retainers (#1) and team-building engagements (#2). Structurally distinct from staffing agencies and subcontracting.
**Downsides:** Requires upfront investment in network-building and vetting before scale. Each member represents a quality risk to the Innovise brand. Needs a clear quality bar and engagement protocol.
**Confidence:** 84%
**Complexity:** Medium — requires deliberate network-building and a quality protocol
**Status:** Unexplored

## What to Remove or Rethink

- **Custom website builds** (current package) — commoditized in 2026. Standard builds with modern tech aren't a differentiator. Fold into general dev engagement or remove as a standalone package.
- **Monthly maintenance retainer** (current form) — "software support on demand" is undifferentiated. Rebuild as the Fractional Embedded Partner (#1) for strategic clients, or as a scoped risk-monitoring product for lighter clients. Current framing doesn't differentiate.
